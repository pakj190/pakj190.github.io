Instructions

Front Panel:

Magic loop

Chain the width of your top in multiples of 3 (if you want to have a wider top, crochet more chains, if you want a tighter top, crochet less chains)

Put a stitch marker in the last chain you make

Chain 10

Double crochet into the chain with the stitch marker

Chain 2 and skip 2 chains and on the 3rd chain, double crochet

Continue to the end

Once the row is finished, chain 7

Double crochet into the corner of the square from the previous row

(skip 2 chain and double crochet into the 3rd)

Continue down the row

Repeat this until you are satisfied with the length of your top

To make the neck hole, we are going to crochet “shoulder” panels which is basically the same thing but on each end of the sides

When you get to the end of the row, chain 7

Double crochet into the 3rd stitch just like you did before in the body panel

Make about 3-4 squares (the more squares, the more narrow your neck hole will be)

Repeat until you have 3 rows (3x3 squares)

Chain 1 and tie it off

Second Shoulder Panel:

Magic loop

Count 3 squares from the opposite side

Insert hook into the 3rd square corner (9th stitch)

Chain 7

Double crochet into 3rd

Chain 2, double crochet into 3rd stitch until the end

Chain 7, double crochet into 3rd and repeat

This time make sure you have 4 rows x 3

You should have 4x3 squares just like the other side

Finish the Neck Hole:

Count all the squares that are in-between the shoulder panels

I have 10 squares

Multiply that number by 3 which means for me it is 30 and then minus 1 so 29

I have to chain 29 and you chain whatever number you got from the equation

Double crochet into the side that has 3x3 squares

Finish the row with chain 2, double crochet into the 3rd

Now you have your neck hole

Back Panel:

Chain 7, double crochet into the 3rd stitch and repeat

When you get to the chain 29, count 3 stitches and double crochet

Keep going until you have the same amount of rows as the front panel

Tie off and cut the yarn
